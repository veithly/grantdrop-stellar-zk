# Prize Thesis: Stellar Hacks Real-World ZK

## Primary Prize

- Primary prize / track / rubric behavior: First Place in the single open innovation track for Real-World ZK on Stellar.
- Primary prize: First Place open innovation track.
- Backup prize, if any: none.
- Why this exact prize is winnable: The official page asks for meaningful ZK on Stellar and leaves the product category open. The candidate pool can beat primitive demos by making a hidden user condition unlock a visible Stellar result.
- Why we are not optimizing for other prizes: No separate sponsor prizes or tracks were published on the captured DoraHacks page.
- Project language: English
- Language source: DoraHacks page and submission surface are English; user requested Chinese for working instructions.

## Sponsor Primitive

- Primary sponsor/domain primitive: Stellar/Soroban transaction or contract state triggered by a ZK-validated user condition.
- User action changed by this primitive: the user can claim, settle, unlock, post, or redeem a result that becomes inspectable on Stellar.
- What breaks if removed: the product becomes a browser-only demo with no public settlement, ownership, or chain-verifiable result.
- Proof surface: app success screen, Stellar tx/contract state, README commands, and `.hunter/claim-reality-matrix.md` after build.

## Target User And Feasibility

- Target user/community/workflow: people who need a private qualification, bid, score, payment, or claim to trigger a public reward/access/receipt.
- Buyer / budget owner, if this became a product: grant programs, wallets, creators, marketplaces, remittance/payment apps, and communities distributing rewards.
- Purchase/adoption trigger: public chains expose too much context, while reward/payment/settlement systems still need proof that rules were followed.
- Existing alternative: upload documents, reveal full wallet history, trust a private admin, or accept an opaque dashboard.
- Why our demo is more than a toy: the same proof-to-result engine can power grants, sealed bids, remittance vouchers, creator rewards, and proof-backed score payouts.

## 30-Second Demo Moment

- 0-10s hook: "Unlock the public reward without showing the private secret."
- 10-30s user action: the judge changes a private input, signs with a Stellar wallet or demo signer, and submits the claim/settlement.
- Visible result: a payout, pass, score, bid result, or receipt flips from pending to accepted on screen.
- Proof artifact: Stellar tx/contract state plus reproducible proof/verification command.

## Killer Artifact

- Proof phrase: "Hidden condition, public Stellar result."
- Why a judge can repeat it: it uses plain words and points to a visible artifact, not a cryptographic primitive.
- Where it appears: README first line, demo video first 30 seconds, and app success state.

## No-Go Trigger

- Kill if: the product cannot show a Stellar-visible result from the hero action.
- Pivot if: the chosen lane needs regulated partners, production custody, or audited privacy guarantees for the demo to be credible.
- Do not build: verifier dashboard, proof log, compliance admin board, or full shielded-wallet claim.
