Top pick and backup pick

Top pick: S2 Masked Bid Market.
It has the cleanest non-domain product magnet: “I secretly bid; the winner is proven; the seller gets paid.” The first action is obvious, the input mutation is obvious, the 5-second clip is visual, the artifact is a sealed settlement receipt, and the desire pull combines deal-making, competition, fairness, privacy, and money.

Backup pick: S4 Ghost Race Bounty.
It is the most video-friendly and easiest to play, but it is weaker as a “real-world Stellar” product unless the bounty payout is unmistakably the point, not the game. The shortlist itself flags Ghost Race as best replay/playability but weaker real-world ZK theme.

First screen sentence for S2

“Place a private bid; if you win, the seller gets paid without revealing everyone’s prices.”

5-second replayable clip for S2

A judge types a bid amount, clicks Mask Bid, and their bidder card turns into a mask. The auction closes, losing masks stay sealed, one winner card pops forward, and a Settlement Receipt: Paid card appears.

Closest boring clone and how to keep it out

Closest boring clone: a sealed-bid verifier dashboard showing proof hashes, bid commitments, contract logs, and settlement rows.

Keep it out of the demo: never open on the verifier, proof log, contract selector, or admin table. Open on the item, the private bid box, and the masked bidders. Show only the human state change first: bid hidden → winner selected → seller paid. Put proof, public inputs, tx, and contract inspection behind an Inspect proof link after the settlement receipt, matching the guidance that proof/log/benchmark evidence should stay backstage until the product consequence lands.
