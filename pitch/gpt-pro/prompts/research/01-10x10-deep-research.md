1. Required Construct Primer
- official wording or closest official wording;

- research evidence used to define the construct;
- one binding prompt constraint for later ideation.

2. Product-Market Arena
- desire-led cool utility signals;
- voluntary behaviors around privacy, proof, money movement, compliance, credentials, auctions, remittance, creator/business status, or payment trust;
- user superpowers;
- public flex artifacts;
- Tuesday pull;
- serious value behind cool surfaces;

3. Audience Proximity Anchors
- non-domain judge personal stakes;
- familiar first-5s desire/pressure hooks;
- public demo hooks;
- retell lines;
- industry buyers behind human-close arenas.

4. Product Ambition Patterns
- beachhead vs product examples;
- adjacent-case expansion patterns;
- reusable engines observed;
- narrowness traps.

5. 10x10 Autopsy Pack

Provide 10 HACK entries and 10 PROD entries. Each entry must include:
- source/title/date when available;
- source URL;
- user/action;
- state change;
- result artifact;
- ZK/Stellar or adjacent primitive;
- packaging move;
- clone trap or later decision it constrains.

6. Pattern Extraction Matrix

Compare repeated mechanics across the entries:
- judge-controlled action;
- 5-second replayable state change;
- shareable/comparable artifact;
- where ZK is load-bearing;
- where Stellar would be load-bearing;
- buyer/budget signal;
- danger of becoming a proof dashboard.

7. Product Loop Borrowing Menu

List structural moves that later ideation can borrow without copying products.

8. Backstage Submission Constraints
- logs/benchmarks/proofs/manifests likely required;
- reproducibility/disclosure requirements;
- constraints that should not become the product name or first demo beat.

9. Weak Source Rejections

Name weak sources or weak idea shapes found, and why they should not constrain us.

10. Research Red-Team
- evidence gaps;
- likely overclaimed technical paths;
- what must be verified with official docs or code before build;
- what would make a project ineligible or boring.
